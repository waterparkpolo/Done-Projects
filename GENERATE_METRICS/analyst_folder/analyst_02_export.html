Prompt: What are the key requirements for a new Acceptable Use Policy? [grc-policy-draft]

Response: An Acceptable Use Policy should clearly define prohibited activities. A good [grc-policy-draft] will include examples.

Prompt: Can you review this draft of the company's Information Security Policy? [grc-policy-review]

Response: I've reviewed the policy. It looks solid but could be strengthened in the data classification section.

Prompt: What's the difference between a policy and a standard? [grc-policy-framework]

Response: A policy is a high-level statement of intent, while a standard provides specific, mandatory requirements to support that policy. This is a core concept of the [grc-policy-framework].

Prompt: Our SOC 2 auditors are here next week. Can you list the key policies they will want to see? [grc-compliance-soc2] [grc-policy-review]

Response: For a [grc-compliance-soc2] audit, they will almost certainly ask for the Information Security Policy, Access Control Policy, and Incident Response Plan.
